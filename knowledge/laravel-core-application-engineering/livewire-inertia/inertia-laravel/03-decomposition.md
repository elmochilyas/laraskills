## Decomposition Strategy

The knowledge is decomposed to separate conceptual understanding from practical implementation. The boundaries are drawn to isolate:

1. **Core concepts and mental models** — the foundational understanding needed before implementation
2. **Patterns and architectural decisions** — the reusable approaches and their tradeoffs
3. **Performance and production concerns** — operational aspects that affect real-world usage
4. **Common mistakes and failure modes** — practical wisdom from production experience

This structure allows engineers to progressively build understanding, starting with fundamentals and progressing to advanced production considerations.
